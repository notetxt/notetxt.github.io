What it does

Built for the entries you mean only for yourself

More than a blank text box — a proper private journal with the features that make daily writing feel safe, searchable, and yours.

Privacy

Optional password lock

Set a passphrase and your journal entries are obfuscated in localStorage. Anyone who opens the browser sees a lock screen, not your words. No account recovery — only your passphrase opens it.

Logging

Date & time stamped entries

Every entry records the exact date and time it was written. Scroll your list and watch your writing life turn into a personal timeline, one entry at a time.

Insight

Mood tracker per entry

Pick one of seven mood emojis when saving an entry. Glance back at your list and see at a glance how your days have felt — without reading a single word.

Search

Full-text search

The search bar filters every entry in real time — matching words in titles and body text. Find that thing you wrote three months ago in seconds.

Export

Download all entries as .txt

Export your entire journal as a single plain-text file whenever you want a backup or want to move your writing somewhere else. Your words, your file.

Writing

Live word count

Each entry shows a word and character count as you type. Useful for stream-of-consciousness targets, therapy prompts, or simply knowing how much you wrote today.

Getting started

From first entry to locked vault in four steps

The journal is ready the moment you open the page. The lock is optional — add it only if you share your device.

1

Open & write

Click the page, hit + New Entry, choose your mood, and write whatever is on your mind. Title is optional.

→
2

Save the entry

Hit Save Entry and it's added to the list with a date stamp. Your entry is stored in your browser's localStorage instantly.

→
3

Set a passphrase (optional)

Open Set Password in the toolbar, choose a passphrase, and apply it. Next time you visit, a lock screen appears before any entries do.

→
4

Search & export

Use the search bar to find old entries by keyword. Hit Export .txt whenever you want a local backup file of everything.
