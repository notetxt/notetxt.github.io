Cookies Policy

No tracking bugs, just local mechanics.

Last updated: June 21, 2026. Here is a breakdown of why we choose persistent local storage profiles over tracking pixels.

1. What are Cookies and Local Storage?

Cookies are little bits of tracking files dropped onto your machine to help web systems recognize you across platforms. Instead of tracking cookies, NoteTxt defaults to HTML5 Local Storage. This approach sets aside a small, sandbox-style zone inside your browser application to save text elements safely without creating data transfer pipelines back to us.

2. How We Employ Browser Storage

We leverage local storage arrays purely to deliver fundamental user requirements: holding your text documents on the notepad screen, archiving your checklist items on the list-builder interface, and pinning sticky memos across layouts. This keeps your configuration intact whenever you return to the tab.

3. Managing Storage Choices

If you prefer that our tools do not persist your written items between sessions, you can adjust your privacy parameters within your browser app settings. Activating an ephemeral browsing mode (such as Incognito or Private Windows) automatically flushes all local cache layers the second you shut the program layout.
